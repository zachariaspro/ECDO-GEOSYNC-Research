## The Five Papers
### Paper 1: Wobble Extinction
**"Unprecedented Extinction of Earth's Chandler and Annual Wobbles: Evidence for Degraded Core-Mantle Boundary Coupling (1846–2026)"**
Establishes the observational foundation:
- 180 years of IERS polar motion data analyzed
- Both periodic wobble components collapsed to ~2% of baseline
- Annual wobble collapse despite *increased* seasonal forcing (+23%)
- Transfer function failure confirmed — the system converting forcing to response has degraded
- Anti-correlation between components (2010–2015) excludes methodological artifacts

### Paper 2: Directional Forcing
**"Systematic Directional Forcing of Earth's Rotation Pole Toward 75°W: Convergent Evidence from Polar Motion Cusps, Satellite Gravimetry and Geomagnetic Field Correlation (1973–2026)"**
Establishes the directional signature:
- Theoretical prediction from LLSVP physics: 80° ± 15°
- Velocity-based cusp analysis: 72.8° ± 0.6°
- Curvature-based blind search: 79° (p = 1.24 × 10⁻¹⁴)
- Five independent methods converge: **75.4° ± 3.4°**
- Hook rate anti-correlates with wobble amplitude (r = −0.70)
- GRACE gravimetry confirms mass accumulation along same bearing
- Geomagnetic correlation: r = −0.98

### Paper 3: Coupling Quantification 
**"Critical Degradation of Earth's Rotational Coupling Revealed by Transfer Function Inversion and Broadband Polar Motion Analysis (1973–2026)"**
Establishes mechanism and current state:
- Broadband collapse: Chandler (98.6%), Annual (97.2%), Semiannual (93.7%)
- Coupling proxy η = 2.0% — at the boundary of complete failure
- Electromagnetic coupling identified as primary driver (r = 0.97 with dipole moment)
- Cascade mechanism: EM failure → topographic → gravitational
- Five independent observables converge:
  1. Dipole moment correlation
  2. Inner core rotation pause (2009)
  3. Free Core Nutation phase jump (2021–2022)
  4. Chandler phase instability
  5. Geomagnetic jerk-to-LOD lag reduction

### Paper 4: Rotational Multistability
**"Earth's Rotational Multi-Stability: Directional Evidence for a Second Principal-Axis Equilibrium from LLSVP Inertia Structure, Coupling Collapse, and Contemporary Polar Motion Trajectory (1962–2026)"**
Establishes the State 1 / State 2 framework by synthesizing four independent evidentiary streams:
- **Inertia geometry** — Monte Carlo diagonalisation of the combined African + Pacific LLSVP inertia tensor (10⁶ samples × 20 seeds; African mass excess 1.48 × 10²¹ kg, Pacific 1.29 × 10²¹ kg) identifies an I₂ intermediate-moment saddle axis
- **Direct tomographic integration** — S40RTS volumetric integration places the I₂ saddle axis at pole-plane bearing **74.7°** (73.9°–77.6° across parameter sweeps), matching Paper 2's empirical 75.4° ± 3.4° (R = 0.9982) to within 0.7°
- **Wobble-response collapse** (Paper 1) — amplitudes at 2.8% of baseline imply a 35–50-fold weakening of the restoring dynamics confining the pole to State 1
- **Coupling degradation** (Paper 3) — η from 0.67 (2005–2010) to 0.039 (2024–2026), a 94% reduction in the mechanical linkage maintaining State 1
- **Sustained mean-pole migration** — IERS 5-year mean pole from 228 mas displacement (1970–1975) to 394 mas (2025–2026), with 87% of the total aligned to the 75°W attractor; the mean pole crossed the 75°W great circle during 2010–2015 and continues beyond it

**Conclusion:** Earth's rotation occupies a softening basin within a state space that contains a second attractor (State 2) along a specific, identifiable direction. The arc distance from State 1 to State 2 and the dynamical mechanism of any transition between basins are derived in Paper 5.

### Paper 5: Post-Transition Equilibrium Axis Derivation
**"Derivation of the Post-Transition Rotational Equilibrium Axis from the Outer Rotating Body Inertia Tensor and the State II Forcing Direction (1962–2026)"**
Derives the specific geographic coordinates of State 2 from first principles:
- Introduces the **Outer Rotating Body (ORB) tensor** — the whole-Earth tensor with the spherically-symmetric core contribution subtracted (I_core = 7.25 × 10³⁶ kg m², 9.0% of whole-Earth C); the dynamically relevant inertia object under the mediated-Dzhanibekov regime implied by Paper 3's coupling collapse, with fractional triaxiality (C−A)/C amplified to 3.79 × 10⁻³ relative to the whole-Earth value of 3.45 × 10⁻³
- Constructed from four published LLSVP reconstructions (Cottaar & Lekić 2016; French & Romanowicz 2015 SEMUCB-WM1; Ritsema et al. 2011 S40RTS; Koelemeijer et al. 2017) at **5 × 10⁵ Monte Carlo samples per province**
- **I₃ (max moment) axis** at (89.19°N, 10.49°E), **0.81° from the present geographic pole** — the quasi-fluid attractor, State 1
- **I₂ (intermediate moment, saddle) axis** at (0.07°N, 84.69°W) in the Caribbean equator — the tennis-racket-unstable axis separating the State 1 and State 2 basins; its direction from I₃ corresponds to Paper 2's empirically measured 75°W forcing bearing (with a ~9° offset attributed to TPW-direction superposition and tomographic noise in I₂)
- **I₁ (min moment) axis**, African-hemisphere endpoint at **(0.91°S ± 0.56°, 3.42°E ± 1.29°)** in the Gulf of Guinea ~150 km SSW of Bata, Equatorial Guinea — **State 2**
- Euler-equation integration (DOP853, rtol 10⁻¹⁰) from above-separatrix initial conditions set by Paper 2's 75°W bearing under Paper 3's η-scaled damping confirms the trajectory from near-I₃ crosses the I₂ saddle and terminates at the African endpoint of I₁ across all four tomographies
- Endpoint selection robust: 80/80 Monte Carlo bearing draws from β ~ N(75.4°, 3.4°) select the African endpoint; sign-flip control selects Pacific endpoint, confirming deterministic bifurcation by initial ω₁ sign
- **State 1 → State 2 angular distance: 89.98° (≈ 90°)** — a geometric invariant of any triaxial ORB tensor, recovering Kirschvink et al. (1997) IITPW 90° as a first-principles necessity rather than an empirical event-specific number
- New geographic south pole at the antipode **(0.91°N, 176.58°W)** in the central Pacific Ocean
- Post-transition geography places West/Central Africa at the new north pole, the central Pacific at the new south pole, and Antarctica at new mid-latitudes
- **Result derived with no free parameter** beyond the observational record of Papers 1–3 and the published LLSVP tomographic consensus
